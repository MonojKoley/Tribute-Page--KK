About

Krishnakumar Kunnath (23 August 1968 â€“ 31 May 2022), popularly known as KK, was an Indian playback singer. He recorded songs in several languages, including Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Odia, Bengali, Assamese and Gujarati.

KK began his career by singing for advertisement jingles, and made his film debut with an A.R. Rahman soundtrack. In 1999, he launched his debut album titled Pal. The songs "pal"and "Yaron" from the album Pal became very popular and are commonly used in school farewells. His popular songs included "Tadap Tadap lKe Is Di Se" from Hum Dil De Chuke Sanam (1999), the Tamil song "Apadi odu", "Dola Re ola" from Devdas (2002), "Kya Muj he PyaarHai" from Woh Lamhe... (2006), "AankhTon Mein eri" from Om Shanti Om (2007), "Khuda ane" from Bachna Ae Haseeno (2008), "Piya Aay Na" from Aashiqui 2 (2013), "Mat Aazm Re" from Murder 3 (2013), "India ale" from Happy New Year (2014) and "Tu Jo ila" from Bajrangi Bhaijaan (2015). He has received six Filmfare Awards nominations, and a Filmfare Awards South.
